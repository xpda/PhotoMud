Adjusting the Color Saturation

The Saturation command, under the Color menu of Photo Mud Editor, is used to adjust the color saturation of an image.

The color saturation of an image is the amount of color relative to the amount of gray in the image. The more gray, or closer to black and white an image is, the less saturation it has.

The Original and Modified images can be zoomed in or out using the command buttons between the images. You can drag the mouse across an image to move or pan the images. It may be useful to zoom in on the image to get a better idea of the changes being made.
