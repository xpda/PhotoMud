Stretching an Image

The Stretch command is used to fit an image into an are defined by four points.

To use this command, select Stretch from the Image menu of Photo Mud Editor. Next, click on 1 to 4 points where the corners of the image are to be located. The first point is the lower left, the second is the lower right, the third is the upper left, and the fourth point is the upper right corner. The current image will be stretched to fit between the points you set.

A rubber band box shows where the image will be located. If you don't want to use all four points to define the location, press Enter when you are ready to complete the command.

The Stretch command is similar to the Stretch Paste command, but with Stretch Paste an image in the clipboard is pasted into the area defined by the four points.
