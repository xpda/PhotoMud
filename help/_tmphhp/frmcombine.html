Combining Two Photos
 Previous Top Next

To combine two photos, first open both photos in the Photo Mud Editor. When more than one photo is open, you can switch from one to another under the Windows menu or by pressing Ctrl- F6. If more than two photos are open, you will be able to select the second photo to be used with the currently displayed photo.

Combine Photos is used to combine the two photos into a single photo. The photo displayed on the active Photo Mud Editor window, photo 1, is replaced with the combined photo.

If you check the "Scale to Fit" option, photo 2 (on top) will be scaled so it fits inside photo one. If you uncheck "Maintain Aspect Ratio," the height and width of photo 2 will be scaled to fit on top of photo 1.

If you want one of the photos to be converted to grayscale for the combining, you can check that box below the photo.

There are several combination methods that can be used to combine the photos:

·    Add -- The corresponding pixels of the two photos are added together. You may want to adjust the intensity of the photos to produce the desired result. This is sometimes used with the edge detection filters to sharpen an photo.
·    And -- This "And's" each pair of pixels. It is a logical bit operation than can produce some unusual colors for special effects.
·    Average -- Takes the average of corresponding pixels of the two original photos.
·    Divide photo 1 -- Divides each pixel of photo 1 (on bottom) from the corresponding pixel in photo 2. This produces some unusual colors for special effects.
·    Divide photo 2 -- Divides each pixel of photo 2 (on top) from the corresponding pixel in photo 2. This produces some unusual colors for special effects.
·    Exclusive Or -- This "XOR's" each pair of pixels. It is a logical bit operation than can produce some unusual colors.
·    Maximum -- The resulting photo uses the brightest pixel of each photo.
·    Minimum -- The resulting photo uses the darkest pixel of each photo.
·    Multiply -- The corresponding pixels of the two photos are multiplied together. Use this method to add one photo to the white area of another.
·    Or -- This "Or's" each pair of pixels. It is a logical bit operation than can produce some unusual colors for special effects.
·    Replace -- Replaces the pixels in photo 1 with photo 2. This just copies photo 2 onto photo 1.
·    Subtract photo 1 -- Subtracts each pixel of photo 1 (on bottom) from the corresponding pixel in photo 2. You may want to adjust the intensity of the photos to produce the desired result. This is sometimes used with the edge detection filters to sharpen a photo.
· Subtract photo 2 -- Subtracts each pixel of photo 2 (on top) from the corresponding pixel in photo 1. You may want to adjust the intensity of the photos to produce the desired result. This is sometimes used with the edge detection filters to sharpen a photo.

Combine Photos is under the Tools menu of Photo Mud Editor.
