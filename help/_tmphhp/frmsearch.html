Photo Search
 Previous Top Next

The Photo Search, under the Tools menu of Photo Mud Explorer, searches the comment fields and dates of photos in any folder of your hard drive. A list of the matching photos are displayed and tagged.

The Search Text can be one or more words. A search phrase can be enclosed in quotes. You can select whether you want the matching photos to contain all the search words, or any of the search words.

The minimum and maximum dates can be specified for the search. These dates are compared with the internal photo dates, which are normally the dates the photos were taken, rather than the date the file was last modified.

When the search is complete a list of photos is displayed. You can browse through the list, tagging or untagging photos as desired. To display a photo on the full screen, click on the small photo in the upper right corner.

You can click on the Open Checked button to open the checked files and exit, as long as there are not too many files checked to be opened at once.

You can also click on the Tag Checked button to tag or select the checked files and exit the search. This way you can print, copy, email, etc., the checked photos. If the view style for Photo Mud Explorer is in details mode, the checked files will be tagged. If the view style is thumbnails mode, then the checked files in the current folder will be selected.

For example, you could search for all the photos on hard drive that contain "Denali." Then, after closing the search, you could easily create a web page with all the photos of with Denali in the comments field, since the matching photos are automatically tagged.
