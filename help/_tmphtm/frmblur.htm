Blurring a Photo
 Previous  Next


The  Blur functions can be used to remove noise from a photo, and also to soften the look of a photo.

The Average Blur sets each pixel in the photo to average of its neighborhood.

The Gaussian Blur uses a Gaussian filter for blurring. This removes slightly more noise with less blurring.

The Motion Blur introduces blurring in a specific direction, making the photo look like it was blurred by motion.

Rotational Blur is similar to Motion Blue in a circle, and Radial Blur is similar to motion blur away from a center point. You can set the center point for Rotational and Radial blurring in the Original copy of the photo (on the left).

The Median Filter is better than these blurring filters for noise removal in most cases, and can be used to remove halftones an image scanned from a book or newspaper.

The photo can be zoomed in or out using the + or - buttons, or with the mouse wheel. You can drag the mouse across the Modified photo to move or pan the photos. It may be useful to zoom in on the photo to get a better idea of the changes being made.

The Blur command is in the Image menu of Photo Mud Editor.
