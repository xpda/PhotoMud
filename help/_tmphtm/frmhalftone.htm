Converting to Halftone
 Previous  Next


Halftone images are used for printing presses, and may produce better results as copy machine originals for photos. There are several different halftone methods available with Photo Mud:
  • Print -- Halftone designed for printing
  • View -- Halftone designed for viewing on a computer screen.
  • Rectangular - Halftone with rectangular patterns
  • Circular - Halftone with rectangular patterns
  • Elliptical - Halftone with rectangular patterns
  • Random - Halftone with rectangular patterns
  • Linear - Halftone with rectangular patterns

These methods may have selectable angles, and all but the Print and View methods have selectable pattern sizes.

The converted halftone image may appear to have odd patterns on the video screen, because at some zoom levels, certain parts of the halftone pattern will coincide with dots on the monitor. These patterns should not be apparent when the halftone image is printed.

You can zoom in and out on the original and modified photos with the + and - buttons and with the mouse wheel. It may be useful to zoom in on the image to get a better idea of the changes being made. You can pan or move the photos by dragging the mouse on either photo.

The Halftone command is under the Tools menu of Photo Mud Editor.
