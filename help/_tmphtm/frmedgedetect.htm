Edge Detection
 Previous  Next


Photo Mud provides several edge detection filters. As the name implies, these filters are used to detect edges and lines in a photo. The resulting image is usually black with a few white places where the edges and lines are found.

In some cases, such as photo analysis, this black photo is the desired result. In other cases, you may want to add or subtract the detected edges back to the original photo to make it look sharper. The Edge Detection dialog has command buttons for this, "Add to Original" and "Subtract from Original."

These edge detection methods are available:
  • Gradient Directional
  • Laplacian Line Detection
  • Sobel Edge Detection
  • Prewitt Edge Detection
  • Shift-Difference Edge Detection
  • Line Segment Detection

The "Direction" option specified which direction for the filter to search for edges.

You can zoom in and out on the original and modified photos with the + and - buttons and with the mouse wheel. It may be useful to zoom in on the image to get a better idea of the changes being made. You can pan or move the photos by dragging the mouse on either photo.

Edge Detection is under the Image menu of Photo Mud Editor.
