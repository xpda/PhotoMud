Stretching a Photo
 Previous  Next


The Stretch is used to fit a photo into an area defined by four points.

To use this command, select Stretch from the photo menu of Photo Mud Editor. Next, click on 1 to 4 points in the photo, where the corners of the photo are to be moved. The first point is the lower left, the second is the lower right, the third is the upper left, and the fourth point is the upper right corner.

After you have set the points, press Enter or double-click. The current photo will be stretched to fit between the points you set.
   

A rubber band box shows where the photo will be located. If you don't want to use all four points to define the location, press Enter when you are ready to complete the command.

The Stretch function is useful if you need to "square up" a photo, such as an aerial photo taken with the camera pointed not quite straight down.
