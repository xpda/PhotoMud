Red Eye Removal
 Previous  Next


The Red Eye Removal function can automatically reduce or remove the "red eye" effect of flash photography. Sometimes when a person's photo is taken with a flash, the eyes reflect a bright red color and the person ends up with bright red eyes in the photograph.

To remove the "red eye" effect from a photo:
  • Draw a selection around the eyes to be changed. This prevents unrelated parts of the photo from being affected.
  • Select the threshold that removes or reduces the red eyes.
  • Select the transparency of the color that replaces the red eyes.
  • Choose the color and the lightness of that color to replace the red in the eyes.

You can slide the threshold and transparency controls back and forth until you have the desired settings.

You can zoom in and out on the original and modified photos with the + and - buttons and with the mouse wheel. It may be useful to zoom in on the image to get a better idea of the changes being made. You can pan or move the photos by dragging the mouse on the modified photo.

The Red Eye Removal command is under the Image menu of Photo Mud Editor.
